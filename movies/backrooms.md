okay immediately, i wanna start talking about the scene where clark first finds out the backrooms.

i love how the movie captures the "enticing" nature of the backrooms. why does it make you want to keep explore? why do i want to know every corner of a home depot or a grocery store when I go into it? why does ikea feel so naturally fun to explore, until i'm exhausted and i feel like i want to get out but the shortest way is forward?

first the lighting pulls clark forward. go from lighter room with the furniture to darker, while still seeing the light at the end of the hallway. then there is a stop sign? what is the stop sign for? but wait, an open door, this seems more comforting compared to a stop sign in a dark room. okay now there is a voice coming from the left, just as i got into this huge room that is overwhelming me and makes me want to go back, i want to know where the voice is coming from, is there someone here? its a cutout?? okay but where is the power coming from, is it related to the power outage i have in the store?

and it is incredible how the movie just keep going more and more with the scene. cable goes to a room, doors are small, something starts chasing clark and it is constantly pushing him forward to learn more about the backrooms. 

fear is always interwoven together with curiousity. the dark room feels enticing yet scary, the person at the bar feels interesting yet weird. backrooms is capturing this perfectly. and half cut off furniture attached to ceilings, walls and floor, absurdity of the doors and the rooms, they all add to the fearful curiousity of the backrooms. 

clark's relationship with his wife, and the therapist, provides the backdrop for the story. eventhough backrooms as a concept doesn't need a story, having this connection pulls more real people into the backrooms. 

another detail i just realized, is the house where clark's helpers (kat and bobby) live, also shows up in the backrooms. the movie never really answers why backrooms exist, but it is obvious that clark's mental state is influencing the space. why does it still exist after clark dies? what does is mean for backrooms, if the inspiration itself has gone? would it start feeding on from someone else? would the therapist be that person?

i love how the movie keeps making you ask questions and makes you interested.

the backdrop story, with clark and mary, the therapy and the broken marriage, and all the references but never explained pieces, technically wasn't really the most interesting part. but it was necessary. when the pirate man first enters the scene truly, and we get to see that the monster is how the backrooms remember clark, it feels like it should all connect, but it relaly doesn't. what's most interesting is the backrooms itself, and the characters are just flavors to the scene. 

it was a really well executed movie, and i think there is a lot more to explore, and i would personally enjoy a backrooms sequel, where we see more about it. not necessarily learn what makes it work, but rather just pieces and parts of what it truly can do. 

I also really like how movie doesn't continue for much longer after the monster is revealed. it just gives a flavor of what else could happen, and ends soon after.
