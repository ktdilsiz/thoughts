this movie made me cry non-stop. my first movie from kore-eda and it is the perfect story of a poor japanese family and how they adopt a girl who has been abused by her parents.

every person in the household is rough, but full of love. you can see the subtle but meaningful ways they show each other how they truly care. it is a perfect show case of how asian families say 'i love you' without saying it. 

every adult in the movie has a vice and they are all aware of their shortcomings. yet they are not ignoring it or avoiding it, they have almost accepted their imperfections. this shows them as mature characters and makes their connections more meaningful.

when they can barely afford croquettes and they find a crying alone girl and take her in for the evening, when grandma tends to her wounds from the abuse, when they do not get angry when she wets the bed, it is just a string of unconditional understanding and love. and the fact that they are financially poor, shows how usually the ones with nothing are the ones with biggest hearts.

maybe i've been really emotional today but i couldn't even finish the movie yet, i'm halfway through and i needed to take a break from all the small expressions and mannerisms that kept showing their care for each other. when the mom shows the burn scar she has to Yuri and they have a matching burn scar, and when Yuri is scared that they would beat her up for getting new clothes, these are just so difficult to watch.

movie itself has just been insanely good so far and everything is beautiful.
